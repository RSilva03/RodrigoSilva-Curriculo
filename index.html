    • In a collaborative project involving two people as part of the Computer Networks course, we faced the complex challenge of transmitting video in a dynamic network environment, where bandwidth and round-trip times are variable. Inspired by the DASH approach, we developed an application made up of three distinct elements: the Content Server, full of films in various versions, the Player, responsible for showing the films and the Proxy, a key part with producer and consumer threads, optimising the fluid delivery of video segments.   • Computer Graphics and Interfaces

    • This project, as part of the Computer Graphics and Interfaces course, aimed to implement the Phong lighting model with fragment evaluation (Phong shading) and use the dat.gui library for the graphical interface. We developed a scene made up of 4 objects, allowing individual selection and modification (geometric and material transformations), including geometry replacement. The user interface was located in the top left-hand corner, making it easy to manipulate the selected object. There was also a configuration of visualisation options such as back face culling, z-buffering and control of the animation of the lights. In addition, the application enabled adjustments to the perspective projection parameters (fovy, near and far), as well as camera positioning and orientation. It had support for three lights, with configuration options as point or directional light, allowing the user to manipulate their characteristics (intensities). The position and orientation of the light sources were choreographed by the application. The control interface for the camera, lights and visualisation options was located in the top right-hand corner. The technology used in this project was VS Code (Javascript and HTML).
